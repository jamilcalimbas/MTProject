TOURIST SPOTS IN PASUQUIN

KALITUNGAN FALLS

Nestled in the quiet town of Pasuquin, Ilocos Norte, Kalitungan Falls remains one of nature’s hidden treasures. Located in Barangay San Juan, the falls captivate visitors with their raw, untouched beauty. Surrounded by a lush forest filled with moss-covered trees, vibrant orchids, and colorful mushrooms, Kalitungan offers not only a refreshing escape but also a glimpse of the rich biodiversity that thrives in the area. The cascade pours into a clear, cold pool at the base, inviting travelers to take a swim or simply sit by the water to absorb the serenity of the place.

KINGKONG FALLS

Kingkong Falls in Pasuquin is a natural attraction that offers visitors a refreshing escape into nature. It is characterized by its blue waters and its relatively secluded location in Sulbec. While still relatively under the radar compared to more famous falls, it is growing in popularity among locals. The falls serve as both a scenic wonder and a recreational site, perfect for swimming and connecting with nature.

SEXY BEACH

Located in the coastal town of Pasuquin, Ilocos Norte, Sexy Beach is a hidden paradise known for its distinctive S-shaped shoreline, from which it earned its unique name. The beach is blessed with fine sand, calm waters, and a peaceful atmosphere, making it an ideal spot for visitors seeking relaxation away from crowded tourist destinations. Its natural curve creates a scenic view, especially at sunrise and sunset, when the sky is painted with shades of gold and crimson, reflecting beautifully on the sea.

SULVEC DAM

The Sulvec Dam in Pasuquin, Ilocos Norte, is more than just a structure of concrete and earth. It stands as a symbol of progress and hope for the community. With its vast reservoir, the dam provides water to hundreds of hectares of farmland, supporting the livelihood of countless farmers and ensuring that the fields remain green throughout the seasons. Beyond its role in agriculture, Sulvec Dam also highlights the balance between human ingenuity and nature’s gifts. Surrounded by mountains and peaceful landscapes, it offers not only a source of life but also a place of calm and reflection. For Pasuquin, this dam is not simply an infrastructure project—it is a lifeline, a promise of sustainable growth, and a quiet reminder of how water can nourish both land and people.

PUGAGAO COLD SPRING

Nestled in the quiet barangay of Salpad, Pasuquin, the Pugagao Cold Spring has become a refreshing retreat for both locals and visitors. Known for its clear, chilly waters that flow naturally from underground, the spring offers a peaceful escape from the summer heat. Surrounded by lush greenery and a serene rural landscape, it showcases the natural charm of Ilocos Norte beyond its more popular beaches and historical landmarks.
