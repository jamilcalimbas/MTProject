FESTIVAL OF PASUQUIN

Panagsana Festival of Pasuquin

The Panagsana Festival is a vibrant celebration that highlights the age-old tradition of salt-making in the town of Pasuquin. The word “panagsana” refers to the process of producing salt, a livelihood that has sustained families for generations and shaped the cultural identity of the community. The festival not only honors the hard work of the local salt-makers but also preserves the rich customs and traditions passed down through centuries. Through colorful parades, street dances, and cultural presentations, the festival pays tribute to the hardworking men and women who continue this valuable craft. Local artists, performers, and community groups participate enthusiastically, creating an atmosphere filled with music, dance, and joyous celebration. More than just a feast of culture and history, the Panagsana Festival symbolizes unity, resilience, and pride, reminding everyone of Pasuquin’s enduring heritage and timeless connection to the sea. Visitors are welcomed to experience the flavors of local cuisine, witness traditional salt-making demonstrations, and join in the lively festivities that bring the community together in celebration of their shared identity.

Dumadara Festival of Pasuquin

The Dumadara Festival is a vibrant celebration held annually in Barangay Davila, a coastal community in Pasuquin, Ilocos Norte. Rooted in both tradition and faith, the festival reflects the people’s deep connection to the sea and their gratitude for its blessings. At the heart of the festivity is the dumadara, a kind of fish that plays a significant role in the livelihood and diet of the locals. By dedicating a festival to this fish, the people of Davila not only celebrate a bountiful fishing season but also highlight the importance of their seafaring culture. The festival is more than just a feast; it is an expression of thanksgiving. It coincides with the month of May, which is traditionally dedicated to the Blessed Virgin Mary in Catholic practice. Thus, the Dumadara Festival blends religious devotion with cultural traditions, making it a unique celebration of faith and community life. A thanksgiving mass is held as a central part of the activities, emphasizing the people’s acknowledgment of divine providence in their daily lives and work at sea.
